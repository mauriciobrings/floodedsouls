In the map shown below, layers of water bordering the Isarco River represent locals of Bolzano (IT) hanging out by this water stream in a calm spring day. As they are approached, they talk about what fills their lives with meaning. In a sense, their voices animate an overflow on the riverbanks, as their human water bodies talk about the flooding nature of another water body, the river. Do they talk about actual floods? No, yet, they make sense of a flooding occurrence by embodying this natural event through human language, underlining its expressive meaning, and normalizing the act of releasing external pressure while these locals answer the question: what floods your soul?
